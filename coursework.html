Relevant Coursework

The School of Information offers a variety of classes that have taught me many skills that are applicable to the world of Information Technology.

Feel free to ask me more about the classes I have taken!

SI 339: Web Design, Development, and Accessibility:

The purpose of this course is to provide students with all necessary skills for building and deploying web sites as well as utilize existing software tools. This course provides practical and theoretical coverage to learning responsive, accessible front-end programming for Web Design. Topics I have learned include HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, APIs, and the POUR design principles of accessible design.

ENTR 392: Digital Product Design:

The purpose of this course is to provide an introduction to Digital Product Design. After I complete this course, I will have the skills and portfolio to showcase my design knowledge. I am learning the total end-to-end process of discovering a digital product need, prototyping a digitized product and getting feedback to relentlessly improve it.

SI 310: Information Environments and Work:

This course provides me with a foundation for understanding psychological, economic, management and sociocultural perspectives of work in the post-industrial age coming to be known as the Fourth Industrial Revolution or Industry 4.0. In this course, I have learned to recognize and discuss the challenges associated with information environments and work in the post-industrial age along with their implications for organizations, the people who work for them, and other relevant stakeholders. I have explored the design of information systems and their role in digital transformation of work and the workplace. This course focuses heavily on the challenges and opportunities of working at the frontiers of this new industrial revolution.

SI 206: Data-Oriented Programming:

This course has helped me develop core programming and software development skills, to build competency and literacy in important areas that includes basic data structures, debugging and testing, using distributed code repositories, pattern matching, and programmatic gathering and processing of data. Applications in assignments and labs are oriented around data manipulation.
